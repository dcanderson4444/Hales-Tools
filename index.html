Service Call Notes
1
Fill out the Indoor Unit section — year, brand, tonnage, unit type, location, filter info, coil and blower condition, capacitor readings, drain type, and temperature split.
2
Fill out the Outdoor Unit section — year, brand, tonnage, unit type, coil condition, contactor, capacitor readings, and refrigerant status.
3
The Generated Note at the bottom updates in real time as you fill in fields. When done, tap Copy to Clipboard and paste it wherever you need it.
4
Tap Clear Form to reset everything for the next call.
Tips: Selecting Furnace as the unit type hides tonnage and shows BTU instead, and adds an indoor coil section. Selecting Straight AC as the outdoor type hides the defrost field. The blower capacitor and heat strips sections can be toggled on or off with the checkboxes.
Quote Builder
1
Enter the Job Info — technician name, job address, and PO number or supplier. These show up on the PDF and copied summary.
2
Add each Material with a description and cost. Use + Add material for multiple items. The formula automatically applies tax and margin on top of material costs.
3
Enter the number of techs and hours on the job. Labor is calculated at $270 per tech per hour.
4
If refrigerant was added, select the type and enter the pounds. The cost is added to the total automatically.
5
Use Copy Summary to copy a plain text breakdown to your clipboard to paste into a text or email.
6
Tap Save to Drive to submit the quote. This automatically generates a PDF and saves it to Google Drive, and logs the job details to a Google Sheet. This is purely for reference — so that if a quote ever needs to be looked back on down the road, it's there. Not required every time, but good practice to use when it matters.
Formula: Material cost → add 8% tax → divide by 0.70 for margin → add labor → add refrigerant = customer quote.
On-Call Schedule
1
The top card always shows who is on call the current weekend at a glance.
2
The schedule list shows the next 12 weekends. Weekends where someone switched are marked with an orange Swap badge showing who was originally scheduled.
3
To make a change, scroll to Make a Change, enter your name, select the weekend, select the person who agreed to swap with you, and add an optional note explaining the reason. Tap Save Change.
4
All changes are saved live and visible to everyone on the team instantly. The Change Log at the bottom keeps a full record of every change made and who made it.
Note: The schedule follows a rotating order of Chris → Robert → Justin. Swaps only affect the specific weekend selected — the base rotation stays the same for all other weekends.
These tools are meant to help systemize our work and make things easier for everyone on the team. Any widget can be updated or changed at any time — we are always looking to improve. If you have a suggestion for an edit to an existing tool or an idea for a new one, reach out to Chris.
